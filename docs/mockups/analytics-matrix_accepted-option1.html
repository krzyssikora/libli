Teacher analytics — one view or several?

Every requirement in roles.md is a variation of students × course-components × a metric: whole group or chosen students, any depth (course→unit), progress or results. That shape is a pivotable matrix. Below is that single configurable view; under it, the choice.

Click a column → it expands into that chapter's sections/units (deeper = finer scores). Click a student ▸ → that student's own breakdown. Toggle Results → cells show quiz scores/marks instead of % viewed. Switch scope to a Collection → rows span multiple groups.

So — how many views?

1

One configurable matrix (recommended)

Scope (group/collection, all/chosen students) × depth (course→unit, via column drill-down) × metric (progress/results) all in one screen. Single-student view = click a row; comparison = it's inherently comparative. One powerful view covers every stated need; nothing duplicated.

N

Several purpose-built views

Separate screens: group overview, single-student profile, comparison tool, results-by-question. Each can be tuned to its job, but they overlap heavily, multiply what we design/build, and force the teacher to pick the "right" screen first.
