# Groups & collections

Students are organised through three related ideas: **cohorts**, **groups**, and
**collections**. Understanding how they differ makes the rest of teaching in
the platform straightforward.

## Cohorts

A **cohort** is a platform-wide bucket that every student belongs to — and to
exactly one. Think of it as the student's home year or intake. There is always a
system **Default** cohort (shown as *Name (default)*), so no student is ever
without one. Cohorts are managed by platform admins from the **Admin → Cohorts**
menu; teachers mostly use them as a filter when building a roster.

## Groups

A **group** is a roster of students, together with their teachers, tied to **one
specific course**. The course is chosen when the group is created and locked
afterwards. Groups are where day-to-day teaching happens: analytics, quiz
review, and results all run against a group's roster.

Manage them from the top-bar **Groups** list (or **My groups** for the ones you
teach). Create a group with **New**, give it a **Name** and **Course**, then pick
its teachers and students. Save with **Save**. You can **archive** a group you no
longer need — the list has an *archived* toggle to show or hide them.

## Collections

A **collection** is a named bundle of groups that all sit on the same course,
owned by you. Use one to view several groups together as a single combined
roster — handy when you teach parallel classes of the same subject and want one
place to see them all. Create and edit collections from **My groups →
Collections**.

## How they fit together

- A student lives in exactly one **cohort** (their intake).
- A student can be in many **groups**, one per course they study with you.
- A **collection** gathers several of your groups on one course into a single
  view.
